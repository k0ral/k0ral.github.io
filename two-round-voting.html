Two-round voting system

This simulation illustrates a pathological situation of the two-round voting system. It is inspired from this article (in French.)

For the sake of simplicity, let's consider an election with 3 candidates A, B and C, and let's assume that A wins through the two-round voting system.

Now let's assume that in an alternate universe, the same election happens in the same conditions, except that more people vote for A during the first round. Then, it stands to reason that A should also win the election in that alternate universe.

Unfortunately, this doesn't always hold, and here's an interactive counter-example:

  • Reset the simulation: make sure the cursor is in the leftmost position; observe that candidate A wins the election
  • Move the cursor progressively to the right, such that candidate A gets more votes
  • Observe what happens during both rounds
